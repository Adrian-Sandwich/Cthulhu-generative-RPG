# Call of Cthulhu 7th Edition - Complete Ruleset

## CHARACTERISTICS (Basic Stats)

Every investigator has 8 characteristics, rolled as 3D6 × 5, resulting in percentages from 15-90%.

### 1. STR (Strength): 3D6 × 5
- Physical power for lifting, grappling, melee combat damage modifier
- 0 STR = Comatose/paralyzed

### 2. CON (Constitution): 3D6 × 5
- Health, vigor, resistance to poison/disease
- Affects Hit Points (HP) = CON / 10 (rounded)
- Higher CON = better wound resistance

### 3. POW (Power): 3D6 × 5
- Willpower, magic potential, resistance to mental/magical effects
- Starting Sanity Points (SAN) = POW
- Magic pool points also come from POW

### 4. DEX (Dexterity): 3D6 × 5
- Agility, hand-eye coordination, initiative in combat
- Used for dodging, sneaking, fine motor skills

### 5. APP (Appearance): 3D6 × 5
- Physical attractiveness, charisma, presence
- Affects social interactions (Charm, Persuade, Intimidate)

### 6. EDU (Education): 3D6 × 5
- Academic knowledge, formal learning
- Used for knowledge-based skills (Science, Occult, etc.)
- Can be improved after creating character (Age development)

### 7. SIZ (Size): 2D6 + 6 × 5  (DIFFERENT ROLL!)
- Physical size/build
- Affects HP calculation: (CON + SIZ) / 10
- Determines creature reach in combat

### 8. INT (Intelligence): 2D6 + 6 × 5  (DIFFERENT ROLL!)
- Mental acuity, reasoning, perception speed
- Used for analysis, problem-solving
- Affects skill development points per era

## DERIVED STATS

### Hit Points (HP)
**HP = (CON + SIZ) / 10** (rounded down)
- Once HP reaches 0: death or incapacitation
- Taking damage rolls damage dice specified in weapons/attacks

### Sanity Points (SAN)
- **Starting SAN = POW score**
- 0-29: Incompetent, unreliable, paranoid
- 30-39: Mild anxiety, nightmares
- 40-49: Social dysfunction, occasional instability
- 50-59: Normal person
- 60-69: Confident, calm
- 70-89: Hardened, calm, stable
- 90+: Exceptional stability

**Losing SAN**: Witness horrors, fail sanity checks
- Temporary insanity: Roll 1D10, lose that many SAN points
- Permanent insanity: 0 SAN = character dies/flees/goes mad

### Luck Points
- **Luck = 3D6 × 5**
- Spend 1 Luck = Adjust a skill/characteristic roll by 1 point (1-to-1)
- Can't spend Luck on: damage rolls, Luck rolls, SAN rolls
- No opportunity to gain Luck during adventure (precious resource!)

### Magic Points (MP)
- **MP = POW / 5** (rounded down)
- Required to cast spells
- Recovers 1 point per day of rest

## SKILL SYSTEM

### Skill Roll Mechanics

**Roll d100 (percentile dice):**
- Roll vs. Skill% value
- **Success**: Roll ≤ Skill%
- **Failure**: Roll > Skill%

### Difficulty Levels

1. **Regular Difficulty**
   - Success if roll ≤ Skill%
   - Example: Skill 40%, roll 35 = SUCCESS

2. **Hard Difficulty**
   - Success if roll ≤ (Skill% / 2)
   - Example: Skill 40%, need ≤ 20 = much harder

3. **Extreme Difficulty**
   - Success if roll ≤ (Skill% / 5)
   - Example: Skill 40%, need ≤ 8 = extremely rare

### Bonus & Penalty Dice

- **Bonus Die**: Roll extra d10 with percentile dice, **take BEST result**
- **Penalty Die**: Roll extra d10 with percentile dice, **take WORST result**

### Critical Success / Failure

- **Critical Success**: Roll 1-5 = Auto-success (even with low skill)
- **Critical Failure**: Roll 96-00 = Auto-failure (even with high skill)

### Spending Luck Points

Player may spend Luck points AFTER seeing the roll:
- If roll failed: Spend N Luck points to reduce roll by N
- Example: Rolled 54, skill 50 (fail), spend 5 Luck → roll becomes 49 (success)

## COMBAT SYSTEM

### Combat Round Structure
1. **Declare Actions** (attack, dodge, flee, etc.)
2. **Roll Initiative** (DEX-based)
3. **Resolve Actions** (attack rolls vs. difficulty)
4. **Apply Damage** (if hit)
5. **Next Round**

### Making an Attack

**Skill Roll**: Roll d100 vs Skill%
- **Success**: Roll damage dice for weapon
- **Failure**: Miss, no damage

**Special:**
- **Dodging**: Defender rolls Dodge skill instead
- **Armor**: Absorbs damage before HP lost
- **Multiple Attacks**: Can attempt multiple attacks if skill allows (penalties apply)

### Damage & Wound Levels

**HP Loss:**
- Each damage = roll specified die for weapon
- HP reduced by damage amount
- HP reaches 0 = **Death** or **Incapacitation** (unconscious/dying)

**Injury Effects (Optional Major Wounds):**
- Serious injury = lose combat turn, penalty to actions
- Critical wound = must roll to stay conscious

### Weapons Damage

Examples:
- **Fist**: 1D3 damage (3% modifier for STR bonus)
- **Knife**: 1D6 damage
- **.38 Revolver**: 1D10 damage
- **Rifle**: 2D6 damage
- **Shotgun**: 4D6 damage (close range)

## SANITY SYSTEM

### Encountering Horror

When encountering Mythos horrors/cults/insanity-inducing events:

**Sanity Check Roll**: Roll d100 vs. current SAN
- **Success**: Take 0 SAN loss (you resist)
- **Failure**: Lose 1D10 SAN points (or specified amount)

### Types of Insanity

1. **Temporary Insanity**
   - Duration: 1D10 hours
   - Effects: Specified in scenario (panic, violence, catatonia, etc.)
   - Recovery: Automatic after duration, or with help

2. **Permanent Insanity**
   - Occurs when: 0 SAN points reached
   - Result: Character is **OUT OF GAME**
   - Fate: Dies in appropriate manner OR flees into wilderness, never seen again

### Sanity Table (Optional Recovery)

- 0-9 SAN: Severe disorder, institutionalization needed
- 10-19 SAN: Severe psychiatric help needed
- 20-39 SAN: Professional mental health care
- 40+: Can function with support

**Recovery**: 1-5 SAN per week with rest/therapy

## SKILL DEVELOPMENT (After Adventure)

### Personal Interest Development
- Choose 1D6 skills you focused on during adventure
- Add 1D6 points to each chosen skill
- Max skill = 99%

### Advancement
- Surviving adventures grants experience
- Can develop new skills or improve existing ones

## CHARACTER CREATION SUMMARY

1. **Roll Characteristics** (3D6×5 for most, 2D6+6×5 for SIZ and INT)
2. **Calculate Derived Stats** (HP, SAN, Luck, MP)
3. **Choose Occupation** (Professional skills + free skill points)
4. **Allocate Skill Points** (Base skills + 70 bonus points)
5. **Choose Weapons/Equipment**
6. **Write Background Story**

### Occupations Grant

- **Base Skills**: 5-7 typical skills at specified values
- **Skill Points**: 70 additional points to distribute anywhere
- **Equipment**: Professional gear
- **Credit Rating**: Social/economic status

---

## ADVANCEMENT RULES

- Successful skill use = Mark skill (you can develop it)
- At adventure end: Roll 1D6 for marked skills, add result to skill %
- Skills max at 99%
- Education (EDU) can increase by 1D6 per decade after age 20

---

## LUCK IN PLAY

**When You Can Spend Luck:**
- Adjust a failed skill/characteristic roll (1 Luck = 1 point adjustment)
- Only AFTER rolling and seeing the result

**When You CAN'T Spend Luck:**
- Damage rolls
- Sanity checks/SAN loss rolls
- Luck rolls themselves

## KEY RULES SUMMARY FOR GAME ENGINE

- **All rolls are d100 percentile (0-100)**
- **Success = Roll ≤ Modified Skill Value**
- **Difficulty affects threshold: Regular/Hard(÷2)/Extreme(÷5)**
- **Bonus/Penalty dice = roll extra d10, take best/worst**
- **Critical 1-5 = Auto-success, 96-00 = Auto-fail**
- **Sanity loss = Witness horror, roll SAN check, lose 1D10 if fail**
- **0 HP = Dead/Incapacitated, 0 SAN = Permanent insanity (out of game)**
- **Luck = Precious resource, 3D6×5 points, spend 1-for-1 after roll**
